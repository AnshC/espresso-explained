Espresso Explained.

Espresso: Rich, bold coffee brewed under high pressure.

Bold Flavor in Every Shot

How To Espresso.

  • Grind 18 to 20 grams of coffee beans to a fine consistency.
  • Tamp firmly into the portafilter and attach it to the preheated espresso machine.
  • Brew for 25 to 30 seconds to extract 1.5 to 2 ounces of rich espresso.
  • Serve immediately, or use as a base for other coffee drinks.

Espresso is a concentrated coffee beverage with bold flavor, velvety crema, and a rich aroma, brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans under high pressure. It is the foundation of many popular drinks like lattes, cappuccinos, and macchiatos, offering a versatile and satisfying coffee experience. Known for its intense taste and quick preparation, espresso is often served in small, strong shots, ideal for savoring pure coffee flavors or adding depth to other recipes.

Originating in Italy during the early 20th century, espresso was invented to create a faster brewing method than traditional techniques. Luigi Bezzera is credited with designing the first espresso machine in 1901, revolutionizing coffee culture by combining efficiency with a new, robust flavor profile. Over time, espresso has become a global phenomenon, representing both Italian tradition and modern coffee craftsmanship. Today, it remains a symbol of quality and artistry in the world of coffee.
